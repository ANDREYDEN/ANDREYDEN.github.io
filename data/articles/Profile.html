Experience

I started learning how to code in high school and wrote my first game in PascalABC It was Tic-Tac-Toe... 500 lines of code... I regret every single one of them...

Since then I've gained a lot experience and met some wonderful people who helped me along the way. Here is a short summary of my path so far:

  • Worked as a fullstack developer at Vehikl for 4 years building a scalable .NET API, React front-end and 2 mobile apps in React Native and Unity.
  • Worked as a contract game developer for Maple Mana and co-created Spooky Maze.
  • Gained experience with various programming languages: JavaScript C# Dart Python Golang C++ Java.
  • Founded the Google Developer Student Club in Conestoga College in 2020 and led the club until 2022.
  • Helped organize the largest tech meetup in the Waterloo Region - Hackernest.
  • Successfully competed in multiple coding challenges and hackathons (Space Apps (2018, 2019), NSBEHacks, 4x4 Challenge, Hack the North, ETHWaterloo).

Values

In my opinion, one of the best ways to solidify what you've learned is to explain it to someone else. That is why I always seek opportunities to share my knowledge by teaching and mentoring. Change

I believe that change is what drives progress forward. I strive to be part of teams that welcome change, where new ideas are respected and status quo is always challenged.

Challenge

Throughout my career, I was involved in projects that required a lot of long-term planning and architecture while also tackling problems on the fly. One time, I spent a week figuring out the math and planning the structure of a complex interactive 3D builder tool, and I loved it! Another time, I had to re-seed a database 10 minutes before an important demo, and I loved it just as much! I'm thrilled by a non-trivial problem - that's what I like the most about what I do.

Backstory

It all started back in Kyiv, Ukraine, when I was first introduced to computer programming in a seriese of LEGO workshops in Grade 5. This experience layed the foundation of my programming knowledge: creating desicions, loops and handling robot's hardware input using a visual language similar to Scratch. In Grade 6 I was already competing in the Ukrainian LEGO Nationals.

Following that, in high school I chose to focus my learning on the formal sciences and was rewarded. It was the time of the most mind changing experiences and explorations in the fields of mathematics, physics and computer science. Yeah, by the way, that Tic-Tac-Toe game was also created around the same time. After competing in various Olympiads and competitions it started to become more and more clear what I wanted to dedicate my time to.

After graduating highschool in 2018, I found myself studying in the Kitchener-Waterloo area at Conestoga College. During this time I started to understand how I can apply all of my coding skills in real-life projects. Taking part in various hackathons and tech events gave me the opportunity to expand my toolset, but more importantly it allowed me to establish connections with like-minded people who would help me excel in my career.

College experience and connections led me to join Vehikl - a software consultancy based in Waterloo. First as a co-op and later as a full-time employee I was finally able to write code that real people would use every day!
